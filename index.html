VAE-GAN

(2020-2021)

During sixth form I read Generative Deep Learning by D. Foster which got me really excited about applying ML to generative tasks (specifically for images: it's amazing how we can use such rigid rules to produce organic-looking shapes). After participating in Google CodeIn 2019 (for TensorFlow), I wanted to apply what I had learnt to a more difficult project, so I created my own implementation of a VAE-GAN.

The VAE-GAN framework trains a generator by combining the losses from a VAE and a GAN. The VAE loss focusses more on correctly reconstructing the content of an image, while the GAN loss is more focused on creating an image that has a realistic style, so combining the two (hopefully) gives us the advantages of both. I trained the model of my own dataset of faces scraped from IMDB to produce the results above. Results here.
